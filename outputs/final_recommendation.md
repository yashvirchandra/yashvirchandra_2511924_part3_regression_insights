# Final Recommendation

The regression analysis indicates that marketing spend, customer footfall and inventory availability are the factors most strongly associated with monthly sales.

Among all predictors, customer footfall has the strongest positive relationship with sales, followed by marketing spend and inventory availability. These variables are statistically significant and should receive the highest priority when developing business strategies.

Regional analysis shows that stores in the East region generally perform worse than stores in the South region after accounting for the other variables. However, the differences for the North and West regions are not statistically significant and therefore should not be over-interpreted.

Based on these findings, management should focus on increasing customer traffic through effective marketing campaigns, maintaining high inventory availability and continuously monitoring store performance across different regions.

Although the regression model explains approximately 81% of the variation in monthly sales, it does not include every possible factor affecting sales. Competition, seasonal demand, local events and customer preferences may also influence store performance.

Finally, regression analysis identifies statistical associations rather than direct cause-and-effect relationships. Business decisions should therefore combine regression results with operational knowledge and practical business experience.
