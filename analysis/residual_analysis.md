# Residual Analysis

Predicted monthly sales were calculated using the final multiple regression model.

Residuals were calculated using:

**Residual = Actual Monthly Sales − Predicted Monthly Sales**

## Largest Positive Residuals

| Observation |  Residual |
| ----------- | --------: |
| 112         | 123539.30 |
| 104         | 114468.23 |
| 202         | 110775.45 |
| 291         |  98971.54 |
| 125         |  87759.87 |

These stores performed much better than predicted. This may indicate strong customer loyalty, successful local promotions or business factors that are not included in the regression model.

## Largest Negative Residuals

| Observation |   Residual |
| ----------- | ---------: |
| 67          | -162777.14 |
| 45          | -140983.94 |
| 90          | -122167.79 |
| 33          | -119011.82 |
| 306         | -111236.36 |

These stores performed below the predicted level. Possible reasons include operational issues, inventory shortages, local competition or temporary market conditions.

## Overall Interpretation

The regression model explains approximately 81% of the variation in monthly sales, indicating good predictive performance. However, the presence of large positive and negative residuals suggests that additional business factors influence store performance. The model slightly under-predicts some high-performing stores and over-predicts some lower-performing stores, so its predictions should be combined with business knowledge when making decisions.
